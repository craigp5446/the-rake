# The Rake
*A transparency project for tech revenue models and user alignment*

---

Most people have no framework for understanding how the tech products they use actually make money — or who those products are really optimising for. The Rake is an accountability layer for tech business models. Each issue covers one company: how it makes money, who benefits, and where its incentives align or conflict with the people using it.

Everything is grounded in public information. Sources are cited. Methodology is open.

---

## What it is

The Rake scores tech companies across seven dimensions:

- Revenue clarity
- Incentive alignment
- Captivity
- Engagement extraction
- Multi-sided tension
- Algorithmic transparency
- Ownership pressure

Scores aren't collapsed into a single number. The profile across dimensions is more revealing than any aggregate. Every company receives a label — from **Adversarial** to **Principled** — and a spectrum position (0–100) that shows where within that label it sits.

The research is done systematically using a two-phase AI process, reviewed and written up by a human. The framework is public, forkable, and versioned.

## The open methodology

The framework is public because a transparency project with an opaque methodology would be a contradiction. The scoring rubrics, research prompts, and versioning history are all here. Fork it, critique it, improve it.

If you disagree with a score, the methodology is your starting point. If you have new reliable information that affects a company's report, share it — we'll consider an update and preserve the historical record.

---

## What The Rake is not

- Not a legal document or regulatory filing
- Not a claim about what companies do in private
- Not a campaign to get companies shut down
- Not affiliated with any company, investor, or advocacy group

It is analysis, applied consistently, in public, with sources.
