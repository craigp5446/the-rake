### DIMENSION: Revenue clarity

**SCORE:** 2 — Partial

**RATIONALE:** Substack's primary revenue stream—a 10% platform fee on paid subscriptions—is clearly disclosed on the company website and pricing pages. However, the December 2025 advertising pilot was announced through a paywalled interview with a single creator rather than an official company announcement, and the company's evolving stance on advertising (from "busted" to "new possibilities") represents a secondary revenue stream that is not prominently surfaced to users. The shift toward advertising revenue is material but not transparently communicated.

### DIMENSION: Incentive alignment

**SCORE:** 3 — Aligned

**RATIONALE:** Substack's core business model creates direct alignment: the platform earns revenue only when creators earn paid subscription revenue, taking 10% of creator earnings. This structure means the company makes more money when users (creators) succeed at monetizing their work. While high churn rates and creator defections to competitors suggest imperfect alignment at scale, the fundamental incentive structure rewards creator success rather than extracting value through alternate means.

### DIMENSION: Captivity

**SCORE:** 3 — Portable

**RATIONALE:** Substack provides full data export of posts and subscriber lists in standard formats (CSV for subscribers, ZIP for posts), with comprehensive subscriber data including email addresses, subscription status, and engagement metrics. Paid subscription cancellation is self-serve through account settings with no phone call required, and users can pause subscriptions rather than cancel outright. While migrating paid subscriptions to competitors requires more complex Stripe account transfers, the platform does not deliberately trap users or create artificial switching barriers.

### DIMENSION: Engagement extraction

**SCORE:** 3 — Value-driven

**RATIONALE:** According to Substack's head of machine learning, the Notes algorithm is explicitly optimized for subscriptions rather than time spent or endless scrolling—a deliberate contrast with ad-supported social platforms. Users have control over notification settings and can disable email and push notifications; subscribers do not receive automatic emails when creators post Notes. No evidence was found of deliberate engagement extraction mechanics such as streaks, gamification, or reward schedules designed to defeat user disengagement.

### DIMENSION: Multi-sided tension

**SCORE:** 2 — Mixed record

**RATIONALE:** Substack historically served only creators, but the December 2025 advertising pilot introduces advertisers as a second customer group, creating potential tension. Content moderation is largely delegated to individual writers, and the platform's historical approach to extremist content—including an initial refusal to remove Nazi newsletters despite pressure—demonstrates a mixed record on protecting users from harmful content. The platform eventually removed five newsletters in January 2024 after investigative reporting and creator pressure, but only after initial resistance.

### DIMENSION: Algorithmic accountability

**SCORE:** 2 — Partial accountability

**RATIONALE:** Substack has published explanations of its Notes algorithm through its head of machine learning, providing meaningful detail about how recommendations are generated. However, the company's content moderation policies have been inconsistently applied—CEO Chris Best declined to answer direct questions about moderation in a 2023 interview, and the platform initially refused to remove Nazi content despite documented presence of "scores of white-supremacist, neo-Confederate, and explicitly Nazi newsletters." The company eventually acted under pressure, indicating policies exist but enforcement was reactive rather than proactive.

### DIMENSION: Ownership pressure

**SCORE:** 2 — Moderate pressure

**RATIONALE:** Substack is a mid-stage venture-backed company with approximately $190-220 million raised across six funding rounds, reaching a $1.1 billion valuation in July 2025. Key investors include Andreessen Horowitz (estimated 15-20% ownership) and The Chernin Group. The company reached positive cash flow in Q1 2025, reducing immediate return pressure, but the unicorn valuation and VC investor base create expectations for significant returns. The founders retain significant stakes and rejected an acquisition offer from Elon Musk in 2023, indicating some insulation from pure shareholder pressure.

## PERSONA TAKEAWAYS

**For writers/creators:** Substack's business model is genuinely aligned with your success—they make money only when you do—and you can leave with your full subscriber list whenever you want, though the platform's quiet move toward advertising deserves watching.

**For readers/subscribers:** Substack doesn't use engagement tricks to keep you scrolling, and canceling subscriptions is straightforward, but the platform's hands-off approach to content moderation means you may encounter extremist content in recommendations.
